DOCUMENTS REQUIRED FOR REGISTRATION OF LLP

  • Details of proposed LLP
  • Partners related Details
  • Registered Office related details

Registered Office related details:

  • Registered Office Address
  • If owned Utility bill of the Registered office Address and NOC from owner: No Objection Certificate to be obtained from the owner(s) of registered office
  • If rented premises then Rent Agreement/ Copy of Index/ Municipal tax Bill

Details of proposed LLP:

  • &nbsp;Proposed name of the LLP
  • &nbsp;Proposed Business Activities of the LLP
  • &nbsp;Total and Paid-up Contribution of the Proposed LLP

Partners related Details :

  • &nbsp; &nbsp;&nbsp; PAN Card&nbsp;of Designated Partners and Partners
  • Identity Proof : Aadhar card and Voter ID/ Passport/ Driving License of Designated Partners and Partners
  • Address Proof: Latest Electricity Bill/Telephone Bill/Bank Statement with latest Entry of Designated Partners and Partners
  • Photograph of Designated Partners and Partners.
  • Mobile Number of Designated Partners and Partners
  • E-mail Id of Designated Partners and Partners
  • Duration of Stay at present Address of Designated Partners
  • Occupation and Educational Qualification of Designated Partners
  • Place of Birth of Designated Partners
  • Nationality of Designated Partners
  • Amount of contribution of all Designated Partners and Partners
  • Note&nbsp;: In case of NRI or Foreign National, documents of director (s) must be notarized or apostilled

A LLP is a combination of a partnership firm and a company. A LLP can be started by atleast 2 persons. A LLP is a separate legal entity having perpetual succession, with limited liability only up to the Contribution. A Designated Partner or Partner is not personally liable to the amount of the debt and its personal assets won’t attach to pay the debt. &nbsp;

No professional or educational qualification is required to become a Designated Partner or Partner in LLP. Any individual in the capacity of the person, with the sound of mind, can start a LLP. &nbsp;

Any natural person above the age of 18 years can become Designated Partner in LLP after procuring Designated Partner Identification Number (DPIN). &nbsp;

Yes, any foreign nationals or an NRI can become a Designated Partner or Partner in a LLP in India.&nbsp;

No, a LLP cannot invite the general public to contribute in the LLP.&nbsp;

No, a LLP is not allowed to have foreign direct investment in their LLP. &nbsp;

Once, the LLP is registered, it should follow below-mentioned requirements on priority: Enter into LLP Agreement between all Designated Partners and Partners and submit such agreement to MCA after execution. Open LLP’s current account within 30 days after receiving the PAN card. If the Contribution exceeds Rs. 25 lakhs then appoint a Statutory Auditor Depositing Contribution as mentioned while registration&nbsp;

During every financial year, If the Contribution of the LLP exceed Rs. 25 lakhs or Turnover Exceed Rs. 40 lakhs, then the accounts and financial statements must be audited by an independent auditor. If the LLP does not exceed the above mentioned limits, then the accounts and financial statements are not required to be audited by an independent auditor. Subsequently, Every LLP shall file form LLP-8 and LLP-11 as part of Annual Compliance within given time.&nbsp;

What is a LLP?

A LLP is a combination of a partnership firm and a company. A LLP can be started by atleast 2 persons. A LLP is a separate legal entity having perpetual succession, with limited liability only up to the Contribution. A Designated Partner or Partner is not personally liable to the amount of the debt and its personal assets won’t attach to pay the debt.

Is there any high qualification required to become a Designated Partner or Partner in LLP?

No professional or educational qualification is required to become a Designated Partner or Partner in LLP. Any individual in the capacity of the person, with the sound of mind, can start a LLP.

What are the eligibility criteria for designated Partner to be appointed for LLP registration?

Any natural person above the age of 18 years can become Designated Partner in LLP after procuring Designated Partner Identification Number (DPIN).

Is a foreign National allowed to be Designated Partner or Partner of the LLP?

Yes, any foreign nationals or an NRI can become a Designated Partner or Partner in a LLP in India.

Can LLP invite the general public for Contribution?

No,a LLP cannot invite the general public to contribute in the LLP.

Can LLP has FDI or Foreign direct investment?

No, a LLP is not allowed to have foreign direct investment in their LLP.

What are the Statutory Requirements to be fulfilled once LLP is Registered?

Once, the LLP is registered, it should follow below-mentioned requirements on priority:

  • Open LLP’s current account within 30 days after receiving the PAN card.
  • If the Contribution exceeds Rs. 25 lakhs then appoint a Statutory Auditor
  • Depositing Contribution as mentioned while registration
